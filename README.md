# Engineering Leadership Case Studies

## About Me
I am a security, technology, and AI-enablement leader who works at the intersection of engineering, operational risk, and organizational adoption. I build compact, multidisciplinary teams and the systems around them: clear decision rights, practical guardrails, measurable quality controls, and feedback loops that turn failures into durable improvements.

This repository contains case studies about that work, much of which I first prototype through side projects in a safe environment. They are not product demonstrations or claims that one approach fits every organization. They are intended to make my reasoning visible: how I define a problem, align controls to risk, use evidence to make decisions, and determine where human judgment is crucial.

## Case Studies

### [Guardrailed Agent-Assisted Engineering: A Personal Use Case and Experience](./guardrailed-agent-assisted-engineering.md)

In early 2026, I began building a personal-use iOS parental-control application despite having no prior iOS platform experience. I treated that knowledge gap as a design constraint rather than something to conceal. I am transparent about my experience and limitations; the motivation for undertaking the project is documented in the case study.

The project became a bounded experiment in agent-assisted engineering and quality management. Multiple AI coding systems contributed implementation work, diagnostic hypotheses, and documentation, while a provider-neutral governance harness established shared context, development workflows, security invariants, and autonomy boundaries.

The case study examines:
- How I selected a personal, reversible, noncommercial use case with a proportionate risk profile.
- How specifications, architecture decisions, security-sensitive changes, deployments, and merges remained human-controlled.
- How test-driven development, CI checks, branch protections, security scanning, and hard-denied credential paths constrained agent activity.
- How recurring defects were converted into contract tests, codified invariants, and progressively disclosed project guidance.
- Where the controls remain incomplete and why this personal-scale model should not be treated as sufficient for commercial, regulated, safety-critical, or high-availability software.
- What would need to change before this approach could responsibly scale across a team or organization.

The underlying application, source code, household configuration, and infrastructure remain private. The published case study is intentionally sanitized while retaining the decisions, tradeoffs, evidence, failures, and limitations needed to evaluate the approach. I do make reference to some very limited code.

My general interest as it relates to this study is not simply in whether an agent can produce code; I am interested in how an organization can adopt agentic capabilities while preserving accountability, engineering quality, security, and informed human decision-making.
